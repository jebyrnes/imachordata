Tunicate Whiskey

Posted on Jun 25, 2010 at 00:00
From this issue of the Ascidian News comes word of something….well….awesome. Tunicate Whiskey. To wit, hollow out one Halocynthia roretzi. Fill tunic with Soju (korean whiskey). Drink. For some reason, I think this would work really well with Laphroaig or other Islay malts. Or, perhaps, give that nice Islay character to something more generic - spice up your Johnny walker with a little Styela. On second thought, a big Styela is about the size of a shot glass…

Ascidians in Vanity Fair!

Posted on Jun 15, 2009 at 00:00
Yes, it’s time for squirts to take the limelight in America’s most fashionable magazine! I know this is a little old, but in the 2007 article, If You Knew Sushi by Nick Tosches, the author encounters a delicacy, Halocynthia roretzi aka the Sea Pineapple. (That’s right, they’re even on wikipedia now!) “Sea pineapple,” he says. “Attaches to rocks in the ocean. Tastes something like iodine. Sendai people like it.” It looks nothing like a pineapple.
